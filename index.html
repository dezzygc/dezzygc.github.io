About me

Desiree is an ambitious and well-rounded early career researcher and psychopedagogist, with excellent teaching, pastoral and research skills. I am working towards a career in academic pedagogy, with interests in developmental psycholinguistics, childhood adversity and psychiatric neurobiology. My general research interests lie in observational methods in developmental psychology, theory of psychometrics and experimental cognitive science. I am particularly interested in the use of eye-tracking as a window to early language development in developmental disorders and the use of machine learning to advance psychometric and linguistic research . My other areas of interest relate to issues of promoting accessibility and inclusion in further education, particularly in relation to invisible disabilities, mature learners and under-represented socio-economic backgrounds.
